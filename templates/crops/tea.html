Geographical Distribution

Tea cultivation in India is concentrated in regions with favorable agro-climatic conditions including high rainfall, acidic soil, and cool temperatures. These areas produce some of the world's finest teas with distinct characteristics.

Major Tea Growing Regions:

Assam

Largest tea-producing region with bold, malty flavors

Darjeeling

Premium tea region known as "Champagne of Teas"

Nilgiri

South Indian hills producing brisk, citrusy teas

Dooars

West Bengal region producing strong, full-bodied teas

Tea Varieties

Assam Tea

Bold & Malty

Known for its strong, bold flavor and rich, malty taste. Perfect for breakfast blends and traditional Indian chai. High caffeine content and robust character.

Strong Flavor High Caffeine Breakfast Tea

Darjeeling Tea

Delicate & Aromatic

Prized for its delicate aroma, floral notes, and distinctive muscatel flavor. Often called the "Champagne of Teas" with complex taste profiles varying by flush.

Muscatel Flavor Premium Quality Floral Notes

Other Tea Types:

Nilgiri Tea: Brisk, fruity flavor with citrusy notes, excellent for blending
Green Tea: Gaining popularity for health benefits and unique taste profile
White Tea: Delicate, subtle flavor with minimal processing
Oolong Tea: Semi-fermented tea with complex flavor characteristics

Government Support & Industry

Tea Board of India

Statutory body regulating and promoting the tea industry, ensuring quality standards and export facilitation.

Plantation Subsidies

Financial support for tea plantation development, replantation, and modernization of processing facilities.

Export Promotion

Schemes to promote Indian tea in international markets and maintain quality certifications.

Price Stabilization

Measures to stabilize tea prices and support farmers during market fluctuations.
