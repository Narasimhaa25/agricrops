Geographical Distribution

Cotton cultivation in India spans across multiple agro-climatic zones, with each region contributing uniquely to the nation's cotton production. The crop thrives in semi-arid regions with specific temperature and rainfall requirements.

Major Cotton Growing States:

Gujarat

Leading producer with 33% of total production

Maharashtra

Second largest with quality cotton varieties

Telangana

Significant contributor in South India

Karnataka

Known for long-staple cotton varieties

Cotton Varieties

Gossypium hirsutum

Short Staple

Most widely cultivated variety in India, accounting for about 85% of total cotton production. Known for its adaptability to various climatic conditions.

High Yield Disease Resistant Bt Cotton

Gossypium barbadense

Long Staple

Premium quality cotton with extra-long staple (ELS) fibers. Primarily grown in specific regions with suitable agro-climatic conditions.

Government Support & Schemes

Minimum Support Price

Government ensures fair prices through MSP mechanism, providing income security to cotton farmers.

Crop Insurance

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana provides comprehensive risk coverage against natural calamities.

Input Subsidies

Subsidized seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides to reduce cultivation costs and improve profitability.

Training Programs

Skill development and technology transfer programs for modern cultivation practices.
