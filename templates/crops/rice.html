Geographical Distribution

Rice cultivation is practiced across various agro-climatic regions of India, from the fertile plains of North India to the coastal regions of South India. It is the staple food for majority of the population, especially in eastern and southern states.

Major Rice Growing States:

West Bengal

Leading producer with highest productivity

Uttar Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh

Rice Varieties

Basmati Rice

Premium Quality

Known for its distinct aroma and long grains. Primarily grown in Punjab, Haryana, Uttarakhand, and Uttar Pradesh. Commands premium prices in international markets.

Aromatic Long Grain Export Quality

Non-Basmati Rice

High Volume

Includes varieties like Sona Masuri, IR 64, and Swarna. Grown extensively in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, and West Bengal for domestic consumption.

High Yield Staple Food Diverse Uses

Cultivation Practices

Paddy Cultivation

Predominantly cultivated in flooded fields called paddies. This method helps in weed control, nutrient management, and provides optimal growing conditions for rice.

Upland Cultivation

Practiced in some regions without flooding. Requires different water management techniques and is suitable for areas with water scarcity.

Growing Seasons:

Kharif Season

June - November: Main rice-growing season during monsoon

Rabi Season

December - May: Winter season cultivation using irrigation

Government Support & Schemes

Minimum Support Price

MSP ensures remunerative prices for rice farmers and supports procurement for public distribution system.

Input Subsidies

Subsidies on seeds, fertilizers, and irrigation equipment to reduce cultivation costs and improve productivity.

Water Management

Promotion of water-efficient irrigation techniques like System of Rice Intensification (SRI) and direct seeding.

Research & Extension

Agricultural research institutes work on developing high-yielding, climate-resilient rice varieties.
