Geographical Distribution

Millets are cultivated across various agro-climatic regions of India, particularly in drylands and areas with low rainfall. These climate-resilient crops are well-suited for smallholder farmers in marginalized areas with poor soil fertility.

Major Millet Growing States:

Rajasthan

Leading producer with extensive pearl millet cultivation

Maharashtra

Significant production of finger millet and pearl millet

Karnataka

Major finger millet (ragi) producing state

Gujarat

Important pearl millet cultivation region

Millet Varieties

Pearl Millet (Bajra)

Most Common

The most widely cultivated millet in India, highly drought-tolerant and nutritious. Rich in protein, iron, and zinc.

Drought Tolerant High Protein Iron Rich

Finger Millet (Ragi)

Calcium Rich

Excellent source of calcium and amino acids. Popular in South India for making porridge, dosa, and other traditional foods.

High Calcium Amino Acids Gluten Free

Other Important Varieties:

Foxtail Millet (Kangni): High in protein and dietary fiber
Proso Millet (Cheena): Quick-growing variety with good nutritional value
Barnyard Millet (Jhangora): Low glycemic index, suitable for diabetics
Little Millet (Kutki): Rich in antioxidants and minerals

Nutritional Benefits

High Protein Content

Rich in protein and essential amino acids, making them excellent for muscle development and overall health.

Gluten-Free

Naturally gluten-free grains suitable for people with celiac disease and gluten intolerance.

Low Glycemic Index

Help regulate blood sugar levels, making them ideal for diabetic patients and weight management.

Rich in Antioxidants

High antioxidant content helps fight free radicals and supports immune system health.

Government Support & Initiatives

National Food Security Mission

Special focus on millets to increase production, productivity, and promote consumption.

International Year of Millets

India led the UN declaration of 2023 as International Year of Millets to promote global awareness.

Millets Mission

Comprehensive program to enhance millet value chains and promote millet-based products.

Nutrition Programs

Integration of millets in mid-day meal schemes and public distribution systems.
