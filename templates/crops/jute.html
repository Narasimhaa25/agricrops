Geographical Distribution

Jute cultivation in India is primarily concentrated in the eastern states with fertile alluvial soils, high humidity, and abundant rainfall. These regions provide ideal conditions for jute growth with their river basin locations.

Major Jute Growing States:

West Bengal

Leading producer with 75% of India's output

Bihar

Second largest producer in eastern region

Assam

Significant contributor with quality fiber

Odisha

Growing production in coastal areas

Jute Varieties

Corchorus olitorius

Tossa Jute

Produces finer and stronger fiber, widely grown in West Bengal and Assam. Preferred for high-quality jute products and export markets.

Fine Fiber High Strength Export Quality

Corchorus capsularis

White Jute

Produces coarser fiber, commonly grown in Bihar and other states. Used for packaging materials and industrial applications.

Coarse Fiber Industrial Use Packaging

Government Support & Schemes

Minimum Support Price

Annual MSP announcement to protect farmers' income and ensure fair pricing for jute produce.

Jute Packaging Mandate

JPMA mandates use of jute bags for foodgrain and sugar packaging, supporting market demand.

Input Subsidies

Subsidized seeds, fertilizers, and irrigation equipment to reduce cultivation costs.

Sustainability Focus

Promotion of eco-friendly jute as biodegradable alternative to plastic materials.
