Geographical Distribution

Wheat cultivation in India is concentrated in the northern states with fertile alluvial soils and favorable agro-climatic conditions. These regions form the backbone of India's food security and are known as the "Wheat Bowl" of the country.

Major Wheat Growing States:

Uttar Pradesh

Largest wheat producing state with highest area coverage

Punjab

Highest productivity and quality wheat production

Haryana

Major contributor to national wheat procurement

Crop Rotation Systems

Rice-Wheat System

Most common rotation system in Indo-Gangetic plains. Rice during Kharif season followed by wheat in Rabi season, ensuring year-round productivity.

Wheat-Pulses Rotation

Sustainable system where wheat is rotated with leguminous crops like chickpea, lentil, or field pea, improving soil fertility naturally.

Wheat-Oilseeds System

Rotation with oilseed crops like mustard, sunflower, or safflower, providing diversification and additional income to farmers.

Government Support & Schemes

Minimum Support Price

MSP ensures remunerative prices for wheat farmers and supports government procurement for public distribution system.

Input Subsidies

Subsidies on seeds, fertilizers, and irrigation equipment to reduce cultivation costs and improve productivity.

Crop Insurance

Comprehensive insurance coverage against natural calamities, pests, and diseases under PMFBY scheme.

Research & Development

Continuous research for developing high-yielding, climate-resilient wheat varieties and improved cultivation practices.
