Crops in Tripura

Production Trends (2010–Present)

Area under Cultivation:

1,48,381 hectares

Climate:

Warm and humid subtropical climate

Rainfall:

2200 mm

Soil Types:

  • Reddish yellow brown sandy soil
  • Red loam and sandy loam soil
  • Other alluvial soil
  • Younger alluvial soil
  • Lateritic soil

Agricultural Seasons:

  • Aush (1st Kharif, April–June)
  • Aman (2nd Kharif, July–September)
  • Boro (Rabi, September–March)

Rice

Varieties: Tripura cultivates both traditional (Tulaipanji, Gobindobhog) and high-yielding (Swarna, IR) rice varieties.
Climatic Conditions: Adequate monsoon rainfall and fertile soils support rice growth.
Cultivation Practices: Both transplanting and direct seeding are practiced; wetland cultivation is common.
Land Preparation: Plowing, leveling, and puddling for a suitable seedbed.
Irrigation: Rainfed is common, but tube wells and traditional bamboo drip irrigation supplement water.
Harvesting: November–February, depending on variety and planting time.
Government Support: Subsidies for seeds, fertilizers, machinery, and MSP procurement.

Rubber

Growing Regions: Major plantations in West Tripura, South Tripura, and Dhalai.
Varieties: Hevea brasiliensis (rubber tree), with various clones and hybrids.
Cultivation Practices: Land preparation, planting, tapping, fertilization, and pest management.
Tapping: Latex is collected by tapping the tree trunk, usually every alternate day.
Processing: Latex is processed into sheets, blocks, and concentrate for sale.
Government Support: Subsidies for planting material, land development, and technical training.
Employment: Rubber cultivation provides significant rural employment and supports ancillary industries.

Tea

Growing Regions: North Tripura and Dhalai districts.
Varieties: Orthodox and CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl) teas; green, black, and white teas.
Cultivation Practices: Land preparation, planting, pruning, plucking, and processing.
Processing: Withering, rolling, oxidation, drying, and sorting to produce different tea types.
Employment: Tea gardens provide livelihoods for thousands of rural families.
Government Support: Subsidies for replanting, mechanization, and welfare programs.
Market and Export: Tripura tea is sold domestically and exported, known for its unique flavor and quality.
