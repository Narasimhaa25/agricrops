State Overview

Climate

Tropical monsoon climate with moderate to high rainfall in coastal and Malnad regions, semi-arid conditions in the interior.

Soil Types

Red soil (dominant), laterite soil in coastal areas, black soil in northern districts, and alluvial soil in river valleys.

Irrigation

Well-developed irrigation network with major projects like Krishna, Cauvery, and Tungabhadra river systems.

Agricultural Zones

Ten agro-climatic zones ranging from coastal to hill zones, each with specific crop patterns and farming systems.

Agricultural Profile

Karnataka is one of India's leading agricultural states, known for its diverse cropping patterns and innovative farming practices. The state's varied agro-climatic conditions support the cultivation of food grains, commercial crops, plantation crops, and horticulture, making it a significant contributor to India's agricultural economy.

Crop Diversity

Cultivation of over 200 different crops including cereals, pulses, oilseeds, spices, and plantation crops.

Coffee Capital

Largest coffee producer in India, contributing 70% of the country's coffee production.

Horticultural Hub

Leading producer of fruits, vegetables, flowers, and spices with modern post-harvest infrastructure.

Technology Adoption

Pioneer in precision agriculture, drip irrigation, and biotechnology applications in farming.

Major Crops

Rice (Paddy)

Primary Staple

Rice is a major crop in Karnataka, especially in coastal, Malnad, and irrigated regions. The state grows both traditional and high-yielding varieties, with cultivation in both Kharif and Rabi seasons depending on water availability.

Season: Kharif (June-October), Rabi (November-March)
Major Districts: Raichur, Bellary, Mandya, Shimoga, Dakshina Kannada
Varieties: BPT-5204, Jaya, IR-64, Intan, Rasi
Irrigation: Canal irrigation, bore wells, tank irrigation
Productivity: 3,500-4,000 kg/hectare
Processing: Modern rice mills and parboiling units

Maize

Commercial Crop

Karnataka is one of India's top maize-producing states. Maize is grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons and serves multiple purposes including food, fodder, and industrial applications.

Season: Kharif (June-October), Rabi (November-March)
Major Districts: Davanagere, Haveri, Chitradurga, Belagavi
Uses: Food grain, poultry feed, starch industry
Varieties: Hybrid varieties, composite varieties
Productivity: 4,500-5,500 kg/hectare
Export: Significant export to neighboring states

Coffee

Plantation Crop

Karnataka is India's largest coffee producer, contributing about 70% of the country's coffee production. The Western Ghats provide ideal conditions for both Arabica and Robusta coffee cultivation.

Major Regions: Coorg, Chikmagalur, Hassan, Shimoga
Varieties: Arabica (60%), Robusta (40%)
Altitude: 1000-1500 meters above sea level
Climate: Cool, humid climate with well-distributed rainfall
Processing: Wet and dry processing methods
Export Quality: Premium quality coffee for international markets

Sugarcane

Industrial Crop

Sugarcane is an important commercial crop in Karnataka, supporting a well-developed sugar industry. The state has numerous sugar mills and is known for high productivity and quality.

Major Districts: Belagavi, Bagalkot, Mandya, Mysuru
Season: Annual crop (12-18 months)
Varieties: Co-86032, Co-94012, Co-2001-13
Irrigation: Drip irrigation, furrow irrigation
Processing: 60+ sugar mills across the state
By-products: Bagasse for paper and power generation

Pulses

Protein Source

Karnataka is a major pulse-producing state, growing various legumes that contribute to protein security and soil health. The state's diverse agro-climatic conditions support multiple pulse crops.

Major Pulses: Tur, Gram, Moong, Urad, Field pea
Kharif Pulses: Tur, Moong, Urad
Rabi Pulses: Gram, Field pea, Lentil
Major Districts: Gulbarga, Raichur, Bijapur, Dharwad
Soil Benefits: Nitrogen fixation, organic matter addition
Market: Strong domestic and export demand

Spices

High Value Crop

Karnataka is a major spice-producing state, known for cardamom, pepper, coriander, turmeric, and other spices. The Western Ghats provide ideal conditions for spice cultivation.

Major Spices: Cardamom, Pepper, Coriander, Turmeric
Cardamom Regions: Coorg, Chikmagalur (hill stations)
Pepper Cultivation: Western Ghats, shade-grown
Processing: Modern processing and packaging facilities
Export: Significant export earnings
Quality: Premium quality with geographical indications

Government Initiatives & Support

Raitha Samparka Kendras

One-stop service centers providing agricultural inputs, credit, insurance, and technical support to farmers.

Bhoochetana Scheme

Comprehensive soil health improvement program with micronutrient management and organic farming promotion.

Krishi Bhagya Scheme

Irrigation development program focusing on bore wells, farm ponds, and micro-irrigation systems.

Yashaswini Scheme

Cooperative health insurance scheme for farmers and rural population providing affordable healthcare.

Precision Agriculture

Promotion of modern farming techniques including GPS-guided machinery and variable rate technology.

Horticulture Development

Support for fruit and vegetable cultivation, post-harvest infrastructure, and value addition.
