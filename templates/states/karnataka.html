Production Trends (2010–Present)

Area under Cultivation:

98.95 lakh hectares

Tropical monsoon climate with moderate to high rainfall in coastal and Malnad regions, and semi-arid conditions in the interior.

600–4000 mm (varies by region)

  • Red Soil

  • Kharif (June–October)
  • Rabi (October–March)
  • Zaid (March–June)

Overview

Karnataka is one of the leading agricultural states in India, with a diverse range of crops grown due to its varied climate and soil types. The state is known for both food and cash crops, horticulture, and plantation crops.

Paddy (Rice)

Paddy is a major crop in Karnataka, especially in the coastal, Malnad, and irrigated regions. The crop is mainly grown during the Kharif season, but in some areas, it is also cultivated in the Rabi season. Major rice-growing districts include Raichur, Bellary, Mandya, Shimoga, and Dakshina Kannada.

Maize

Karnataka is one of the top maize-producing states in India. Maize is grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons and is used for food, fodder, and industrial purposes. Major maize-growing districts are Davanagere, Haveri, Chitradurga, and Belagavi.

Pulses

Karnataka is a major producer of pulses such as tur (pigeon pea), gram (chickpea), moong (green gram), and urad (black gram). Pulses are grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons, especially in the dry and semi-arid regions.

Oilseeds

Oilseeds like groundnut, sunflower, safflower, and soybean are important crops in Karnataka. Groundnut is mainly grown in the southern and central districts, while sunflower and safflower are cultivated in the northern dry zones.

Vegetables

Karnataka is a leading producer of vegetables such as tomatoes, onions, brinjal (eggplant), beans, carrots, and leafy greens. Vegetable cultivation is prominent in districts like Bengaluru Rural, Kolar, Belagavi, and Dharwad.

Plantation and Horticulture Crops

Karnataka is famous for its plantation crops such as coffee, tea, arecanut, coconut, and spices (pepper, cardamom). The Western Ghats and Malnad regions are ideal for coffee and spices, while arecanut and coconut are grown in coastal and riverine tracts.
