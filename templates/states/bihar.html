Agricultural Profile

Climate

Subtropical climate with hot summers and cold winters. Favorable monsoon conditions supporting diverse crop cultivation throughout the year.

Rainfall Pattern

Annual precipitation of 1000-1500mm, with monsoon season providing essential water for Kharif crops and irrigation for Rabi crops.

Soil Diversity

Five major soil types: Alluvial soil (Khadar and Bhangar), Piedmont swamp soil, Terai soil, Balsundari soil, and Tal soil.

Cropping Seasons

Kharif: May–October
Rabi: November–March
Zaid: March–June

Agricultural Overview

Bihar's agriculture is characterized by its fertile alluvial soil and abundant groundwater resources in the Gangetic plains. The state supports three distinct cropping seasons and produces a diverse range of crops including rice, wheat, maize, pulses, and oilseeds. The favorable climate and extensive river network provide excellent conditions for both irrigated and rainfed agriculture, making Bihar a significant contributor to India's food grain production.

Gangetic Plains

Fertile alluvial soils deposited by rivers supporting intensive agriculture

Water Resources

Extensive river network and groundwater for irrigation and crop cultivation

Triple Cropping

Three cropping seasons maximizing land utilization and agricultural productivity

Major Crops of Bihar

Rice

Staple Crop
Production Status: Leading rice-producing state with extensive cultivation in fertile Gangetic plains
Popular Varieties: Basmati, Sona Masuri, and other local varieties suited to regional conditions
Irrigation Network: Well-irrigated fields utilizing rivers, canals, ponds, and water bodies
Geographic Advantage: Ganges river and tributaries providing ample water for rice cultivation
Cultural Importance: Staple food crop central to local diet and food security
Seasonal Pattern: Primarily Kharif cultivation with some areas supporting multiple crops

Maize

Commercial Crop
Cultivation Spread: Widespread cultivation contributing significantly to state's agricultural output
Seasonal Cultivation: Major Kharif crop sown during monsoon season for optimal growth
Monsoon Dependency: Relies on monsoon rains for germination and early growth stages
Multiple Applications: Human consumption, animal feed, and industrial processing
Livestock Industry: Important ingredient in livestock and poultry feed production
Economic Importance: Significant contribution to farmer income and state agricultural economy

Wheat

Staple Crop
Cultivation Season: Prominent Rabi crop grown during winter months (November-April)
Climate Requirements: Cool winter temperatures conducive to optimal wheat cultivation
Food Security: Major staple food grain essential for regional food security
Irrigation Support: Relies on irrigation during dry winter months for consistent growth
Productivity Focus: Emphasis on high-yielding varieties and improved cultivation practices
Market Importance: Significant contribution to state's food grain production and trade

Pulses

Protein Crops
Variety Range: Lentils (masoor), chickpeas (chana), pigeon peas (arhar), green gram (moong)
Seasonal Cultivation: Both Rabi and Kharif seasons depending on specific pulse variety
Nutritional Importance: Essential protein source addressing nutritional needs of population
Economic Significance: Important component of state's agricultural economy and farmer income
Crop Examples: Chickpeas (Rabi), pigeon peas and green gram (Kharif) cultivation
Food Security: Critical for protein security and balanced nutrition in rural areas

Oilseeds

Commercial Crop
Primary Crops: Mustard and soybeans grown for oil extraction and processing
Cultural Preference: Mustard oil particularly popular in Bihar cuisine and cooking
Seasonal Pattern: Mustard (Rabi season), soybeans (Kharif season) cultivation
Crop Rotation: Integrated in rotation systems for soil fertility and disease management
Sustainable Practices: Part of sustainable farming systems enhancing soil health
Market Demand: Strong local demand for edible oils and oil-based products

Government Support & Initiatives

Irrigation Development

Expansion of irrigation infrastructure including canals, tube wells, and water management systems for enhanced productivity.

Seed Distribution

Supply of high-yielding variety seeds and improved planting material to farmers for better crop production.

Fertilizer Subsidies

Comprehensive fertilizer subsidy programs to reduce input costs and improve soil fertility management.

Crop Insurance

Insurance coverage against natural calamities, pests, and diseases under various government schemes.

Market Infrastructure

Development of storage facilities, processing units, and market yards for better price realization.

Technology Transfer

Extension services and training programs for modern farming techniques and best practices adoption.
