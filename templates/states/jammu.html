Agricultural Profile

Jammu and Kashmir's agriculture is characterized by its unique geographical diversity, supporting cultivation from subtropical plains to temperate valleys. The region is globally renowned for producing the world's finest saffron and premium Basmati rice, alongside traditional cereals and pulses that form the backbone of local food security.

Saffron Excellence

Pampore region produces world's finest saffron with GI tag, commanding premium prices in international markets.

Premium Rice

Cultivation of aromatic Basmati and traditional rice varieties in the fertile valleys of Kashmir.

Diverse Agro-climates

Multiple agro-climatic zones supporting varied crop cultivation from plains to high-altitude regions.

Traditional Systems

Time-tested farming practices adapted to local conditions, maintaining ecological balance and sustainability.

Major Crops

Saffron

Golden Spice

Kashmir saffron, grown primarily in Pampore, is considered the world's finest quality saffron. The unique climate and soil conditions of the region produce saffron with exceptional aroma, flavor, and medicinal properties, earning it Geographical Indication status.

Primary Region: Pampore (Pulwama district)
Variety: Crocus sativus (Kashmiri Lachha Saffron)
Harvest Season: October-November (autumn flowering)
Climate Requirement: Cold winters, dry summers
Harvesting: Hand-picked flowers, three stigmas per flower
Global Status: GI tagged, premium international markets

Rice (Basmati)

Premium Grain

Rice cultivation in Jammu and Kashmir includes both premium Basmati varieties and traditional local varieties. The fertile valleys and adequate water supply from rivers and lakes create ideal conditions for high-quality rice production.

Season: Kharif (June-November)
Major Areas: Kashmir valley, Jammu plains
Varieties: Basmati, Mushk Budji, Kamad, China-1039
Irrigation: Rivers, lakes, and traditional water channels
Quality Features: Aromatic, long grain, excellent cooking quality
Cultural Importance: Staple food and traditional cuisine base

Wheat

Food Grain

Wheat is a crucial Rabi crop grown across different altitudes in Jammu and Kashmir. The crop is well-adapted to the cold winter conditions and provides essential food security to the region.

Season: Rabi (October-May)
Growing Areas: Jammu plains to Kashmir valleys
Varieties: Local varieties adapted to cold conditions
Altitude Range: Plains to higher elevations
Irrigation: Rivers, canals, and traditional systems
Usage: Local consumption and traditional food products

Pulses

Protein Source

Various pulses are cultivated across Jammu and Kashmir, providing essential protein in the local diet. These crops also contribute to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation and are important for crop rotation systems.

Major Varieties: Masoor, Chana, Moong, Urad, Rajma
Season: Primarily Rabi crops
Climate Adaptation: Cool temperate winters
Soil Benefits: Nitrogen fixation, soil fertility improvement
Traditional Use: Local cuisine and protein source
Market: Local consumption and regional trade
