Agricultural Profile

Climate

Semi-arid with predominantly hot and dry conditions. Monsoon season from June to September brings essential rainfall for crop cultivation.

Rainfall Pattern

Average annual precipitation of 755mm (29.7 inches), with most rainfall concentrated during the monsoon months.

Soil Diversity

Four major soil types: Red Soils (Chelka and Dubba), Black Soil, Laterite Soil, and Alluvial Soil supporting diverse crops.

Cropping Seasons

Kharif (Vaanakalam): June–November
Rabi (Yasangi): November–May

Agricultural Overview

Telangana's agriculture represents a dynamic blend of traditional farming practices and modern agricultural innovations. The state's farming system is characterized by a strategic mix of irrigated and rainfed cultivation, supporting a diverse range of crops that contribute significantly to both state and national food security.

Irrigation Infrastructure

Advanced canal systems, tube wells, bore wells, and traditional tanks

Government Support

Comprehensive subsidies, extension services, and market linkage programs

Crop Diversity

Rice, maize, cotton, jowar, pulses, oilseeds, and horticultural crops

Major Crops of Telangana

Rice

Staple Crop
Cultivation Area: Substantial coverage in both irrigated and rainfed fields
Popular Varieties: Sona Masuri, BPT 5204, MTU 1010, Swarna
Climate Requirements: Tropical conditions with hot summers and monsoon rains
Irrigation Sources: Canals, tube wells, bore wells, and traditional tanks
Cultivation Methods: Both transplanting and direct seeding practices
Market Reach: Strong domestic trade with significant export potential

Maize (Corn)

Commercial Crop
Cultivation Spread: Widespread in both irrigated and rainfed areas
Hybrid Varieties: DKC 9090, DKC 9141, DKC 9191
Climate Adaptation: Thrives in hot summers with moderate to low rainfall
Irrigation Systems: Rainfed and irrigated; drip and sprinkler systems
Crop Rotation: Rotated with cotton, pulses, and sorghum
Multiple Uses: Human consumption, animal feed, industrial processing

Cotton

Cash Crop
Production Status: Leading cotton producer, especially in drier regions
Varieties Grown: DCH 32, Bunny, Mallika, Bt cotton varieties
Ideal Conditions: Hot, dry climate with low humidity
Water Management: Mostly rainfed with increasing drip and sprinkler irrigation
Pest Control: Integrated Pest Management, Bt cotton, biological controls
Market Access: Strong domestic and export markets

Jowar (Sorghum)

Drought Resistant
Cultivation Areas: Widespread in semi-arid and arid regions
Varieties: M 35-1, CSV 20F, SPV 462
Climate Resilience: Excellent drought and heat tolerance
Water Requirements: Mostly rainfed, some Rabi season irrigation
Rotation System: Rotated with cotton, maize, and pulse crops
Utilization: Food grain, animal fodder, and industrial applications

Government Support & Initiatives

Input Subsidies

Comprehensive subsidies on seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, and agricultural machinery to reduce farming costs.

Extension Services

Technical guidance, training programs, and modern farming technique dissemination to farmers.

Market Linkages

Direct market access, fair price mechanisms, and value chain development for better farmer income.

Crop Insurance

Comprehensive insurance coverage against natural calamities, pests, and diseases.
