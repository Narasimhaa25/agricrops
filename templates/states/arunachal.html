Agricultural Profile

Climate

Highly varied from hot and humid at lower altitudes to extremely cold in higher regions. Dense forest coverage with swampy conditions in eastern sections.

Rainfall Pattern

Heavy precipitation ranging from 2500-3000mm annually, supporting diverse agricultural practices across different elevation zones.

Soil Diversity

Five major soil types: Mountain soils, Hill soils, Alluvial soils, Loamy soils, and Laterite soils adapted to varied topography.

Cultivation Systems

Traditional shifting agriculture prevalent

Agricultural Overview

Arunachal Pradesh's agriculture is characterized by its unique topographical diversity and traditional farming systems. The state practices both settled agriculture in valleys and traditional Jhum (shifting) cultivation in hilly terrains. This northeastern state supports diverse agro-climatic conditions due to varied elevation and supports cultivation of both staple foods and cash crops adapted to mountain agriculture.

Jhum Cultivation

Traditional shifting cultivation system with 3-6 year rotation cycles in hilly areas

Elevation Farming

Multi-tier agriculture from valley floors to high-altitude mountain slopes

Crop Diversity

Upland rice, maize, millets, ginger, turmeric, and traditional vegetables

Major Crops of Arunachal Pradesh

Upland Rice

Staple Crop
Cultivation System: Primary crop in Jhum cultivation, grown in association with other crops
Varieties: Sali, Bora, and traditional Jhum varieties adapted to local conditions
Growing Conditions: Rainfed cultivation in hilly terrains and valley areas
Cultural Significance: Staple food crop central to local food security and traditions
Intercropping: Grown with maize, finger millet, beans, and vegetables
Productivity: Lower yields due to traditional methods but sustainable practices

Millets

Climate Resilient
Adaptability: Highly resilient to varying climatic conditions and challenging terrains
Cultivation Areas: Grown in both hilly regions and valley areas across the state
Agricultural Diversity: Contributes to crop diversification and farming system resilience
Nutritional Value: Important source of nutrition and food security for local communities
Traditional Varieties: Finger millet and other indigenous varieties well-suited to local conditions
Sustainable Farming: Supports sustainable agriculture in marginal lands

Maize

Commercial Crop
Geographic Spread: Cultivated in various regions including plains and hilly terrains
Climate Adaptation: Well-adapted to diverse agro-climatic conditions of the state
Cultivation Prevalence: Significant contribution to the state's agricultural landscape
Intercropping System: Often grown with rice, beans, and other crops in Jhum fields
Multiple Uses: Food grain, animal feed, and traditional food preparations
Seasonal Cultivation: Primarily Kharif crop aligned with monsoon patterns

Ginger and Turmeric

Spice Crops
Favorable Conditions: Well-drained soils with humid tropical climate ideal for growth
Cultivation Regions: Specific areas with suitable soil and climatic conditions
Rainfed Agriculture: Relies primarily on monsoon rainfall for optimal growth
Economic Importance: High-value crops providing substantial income to farmers
Processing Potential: Opportunities for value addition and export markets

Government Support & Initiatives

Sustainable Agriculture

Promotion of sustainable farming practices to replace traditional Jhum cultivation with settled agriculture methods.

Horticulture Development

Focus on developing horticultural crops, spices, and cash crops suitable for mountain agriculture.

Organic Farming

Encouragement of organic farming practices leveraging the state's natural advantages and traditional knowledge.

Infrastructure Development

Improvement of rural infrastructure, market connectivity, and storage facilities for agricultural products.

Technology Transfer

Introduction of appropriate technology and modern farming techniques suitable for hill agriculture.

Farmer Training

Capacity building programs for farmers on improved cultivation practices and crop diversification.

Challenges & Opportunities

Topographical Challenges

Difficult terrain and limited accessibility affecting agricultural development and market access

Sustainable Transition

Moving from traditional Jhum to sustainable settled agriculture while preserving cultural practices

Organic Potential

Natural advantages for organic farming and high-value crop production for niche markets

Climate Resilience

Developing climate-smart agriculture practices adapted to mountain ecosystems
