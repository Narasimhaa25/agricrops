State Overview

Climate

Temperate to tropical climate with high rainfall and humidity, creating ideal conditions for diverse crop cultivation.

Alluvial soil in valleys, red and yellow soils on slopes, laterite soils in plateaus, and peaty soils in wetlands.

Agricultural Profile

Meghalaya's agriculture is characterized by its diverse agro-climatic conditions and traditional farming practices adapted to the hilly terrain. The state's high rainfall and unique topography support the cultivation of various crops, with agriculture playing a crucial role in providing livelihoods to a significant portion of the population.

High Rainfall Agriculture

Abundant rainfall supporting diverse crop cultivation and unique agricultural ecosystems.

Spice Production

Significant production of ginger and turmeric with high quality and market demand.

Citrus Excellence

Famous for high-quality oranges, particularly from the Sohra (Cherrapunji) region.

Sustainable Practices

Traditional sustainable farming methods adapted to hilly terrain and environmental conservation.

Major Crops

Rice

Primary Staple

Rice is cultivated across different regions of Meghalaya, with both upland and lowland varieties adapting to the diverse agro-climatic conditions. The state's high rainfall and hilly terrain support unique rice cultivation systems.

Seasons: Kharif (June-October), Rabi (November-March)
Cultivation Systems: Upland rice on slopes, lowland rice in valleys
Varieties: Indigenous varieties and improved high-yielding varieties
Traditional Practices: Shifting (jhum) cultivation in some areas
Terrace Farming: Employed in hilly areas for water management
Biodiversity: Indigenous rice varieties contribute to local biodiversity

Maize

Food & Fodder

Maize is grown in various regions of Meghalaya, adapting well to the state's high rainfall and hilly terrain. It serves multiple purposes and is often integrated into traditional farming systems.

Season: Kharif (June-September)
Cultivation Areas: Both upland and lowland areas
Varieties: Traditional and improved hybrids
Uses: Staple food, livestock fodder, traditional preparations
Farming Systems: Mixed cropping and intercropping practices
Soil Benefits: Included in crop rotation for soil fertility

Ginger

High-Value Spice

Ginger is grown in various regions of Meghalaya, benefiting from the state's high rainfall and moderate temperatures. Meghalaya's ginger is well-regarded for its quality and contributes significantly to farmers' income.

Season: Planted during pre-monsoon or early monsoon
Varieties: Traditional and improved varieties
Cultivation: Monocrop or integrated into agroforestry systems
Propagation: Through rhizomes (vegetative propagation)
Quality: High-quality ginger with good market demand
Market: Regional and national markets, export potential

Turmeric

Medicinal Spice

Turmeric is cultivated in various regions of Meghalaya, thriving in the state's high rainfall and hilly terrain. The crop is recognized for its quality and contributes to farmers' income through local and regional markets.

Season: Planted before or during monsoon season
Varieties: Traditional and improved varieties
Cultivation: Monocrop or in intercropping systems
Propagation: Through rhizomes (vegetative propagation)
Quality: High-quality turmeric with good curcumin content
Uses: Culinary, medicinal, and cosmetic applications

Oranges

Premium Citrus

Meghalaya is renowned for its oranges, especially in regions like Sohra (Cherrapunji). The state's high rainfall and hilly terrain provide favorable conditions for citrus cultivation, producing oranges with excellent flavor and aroma.

Major Regions: Sohra (Cherrapunji), East Khasi Hills
Harvest Season: Winter months (November-February)
Quality: Excellent flavor, aroma, and juice content
Climate Benefits: High rainfall and moderate temperatures
Economic Impact: Significant contribution to farmers' income
Market: Local consumption and regional supply

Vegetables

Nutritional Diversity

Meghalaya cultivates a variety of vegetables including both traditional and modern varieties. The state's climate and terrain support diverse vegetable cultivation providing nutritional security and income opportunities.

Major Vegetables: Cabbage, cauliflower, tomato, beans, leafy greens
Traditional Varieties: Indigenous leafy vegetables and local varieties
Seasonal Cultivation: Year-round cultivation in different seasons
Organic Practices: Traditional organic cultivation methods
Market Access: Local markets and nearby urban centers
Nutritional Value: Important source of vitamins and minerals

Government Initiatives & Support

Spice Development Program

Comprehensive support for ginger and turmeric cultivation including quality improvement, processing, and market linkages.

Horticulture Mission

Support for fruit cultivation, particularly citrus development with focus on quality improvement and post-harvest management.

Organic Farming Initiative

Promotion of organic certification and sustainable farming practices leveraging traditional knowledge systems.

Watershed Development

Soil and water conservation programs including terracing, check dams, and sustainable land management practices.

Market Infrastructure

Development of rural markets, storage facilities, and transportation infrastructure for better market access.

Farmer Producer Organizations

Support for collective farming, value addition, and direct market linkages through farmer cooperatives.

Sustainable Agricultural Practices

Jhum Cultivation

Traditional shifting cultivation with fallow periods allowing forest regeneration and soil fertility restoration.

Agroforestry Systems

Integration of trees with crops providing multiple benefits including soil conservation and additional income.

Terrace Farming

Stepped cultivation on hill slopes preventing soil erosion and optimizing water use efficiency.

Biodiversity Conservation

Preservation of indigenous crop varieties and traditional farming systems maintaining genetic diversity.
