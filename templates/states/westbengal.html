Agricultural Profile

Climate

Tropical savannahs in the south transitioning to humid subtropical in the north. Darjeeling hills experience temperate climate conditions.

Rainfall Pattern

Average annual precipitation of 1600-1700mm, with monsoon season from June to September providing majority of rainfall.

Soil Diversity

Three major soil types: Red and laterite soils (uplands), Saline coastal soils (Sundarbans), and Alluvial soils (Gangetic plains).

Cropping Seasons

Kharif: June-October
Rabi: November-February
Zaid: March-June

Agricultural Overview

West Bengal's agriculture is distinguished by its rich alluvial soils of the Gangetic plains, diverse agro-climatic zones, and traditional farming practices. The state's agricultural system ranges from intensive rice cultivation in the plains to specialty crops like tea in the hills and jute in the deltaic regions, making it a significant contributor to India's agricultural diversity.

Gangetic Fertility

Rich alluvial soils supporting intensive rice cultivation and diverse cropping

Specialty Crops

World-famous Darjeeling tea, leading jute production, and traditional rice varieties

Water Resources

Abundant river systems, traditional irrigation, and monsoon-dependent agriculture

Major Crops of West Bengal

Rice

Staple Crop
Cultivation Area: Extensive cultivation across Gangetic plains with three distinct seasons
Varieties Grown: Traditional varieties (Swarna, Banskathi, Tulsimanjari) and high-yielding (IR36, IR64, Swarna Sub1)
Seasonal Cultivation: Aus (April-August), Aman (July-December), Boro (December-May)
Irrigation Systems: Traditional flood irrigation, canal systems, and modern drip/sprinkler methods
Soil Conditions: Fertile alluvial soils of Gangetic plains ideal for rice cultivation
Production Status: Leading rice producer in eastern India with high productivity levels

Jute

Fiber Crop
Production Leadership: Leading jute producer in India, contributing over 50% of national production
Varieties Cultivated: Corchorus capsularis (white jute) and Corchorus olitorius (tossa jute)
Growing Conditions: Fertile deltaic soils and high humidity ideal for jute cultivation
Processing Industry: Well-established jute mills and processing facilities
Multiple Uses: Manufacturing of sacks, ropes, carpets, handicrafts, and eco-friendly products
Export Market: Significant export potential with growing demand for eco-friendly materials

Tea

Premium Crop
Growing Region: Darjeeling hills renowned for world-class tea production
Climate Requirements: Cool temperatures, high altitude, misty conditions, and well-distributed rainfall
Quality Recognition: Darjeeling tea holds Geographical Indication (GI) status globally
Processing Methods: Traditional orthodox processing maintaining distinctive flavor and aroma
Global Market: Premium export commodity with established international market presence
Employment Generation: Significant rural employment in hill regions through tea gardens

Sugarcane

Cash Crop
Growing Districts: Bardhaman, Murshidabad, Bankura, Nadia, and North 24 Parganas
Varieties Grown: Both traditional and high-yielding hybrid varieties adapted to local conditions
Climate Adaptation: Thrives in tropical/subtropical climate with well-distributed rainfall
Irrigation Methods: Surface irrigation, drip systems, and sprinkler irrigation
Processing Industry: Sugar mills producing sugar, jaggery, and ethanol for various uses
Economic Impact: Important source of rural income and industrial development

Government Support & Initiatives

Kisan Credit Card

Easy access to agricultural credit for farmers to purchase inputs, equipment, and meet cultivation expenses.

Crop Diversification

Promotion of alternative crops, horticulture, and value-added agriculture for better income generation.

Irrigation Infrastructure

Development of canal systems, tube wells, and water conservation structures for assured irrigation.

Market Linkages

Establishment of farmer producer organizations, direct marketing, and value chain development programs.
