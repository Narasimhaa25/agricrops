Crops in West Bengal

Production Trends (2010–Present)

Area under Cultivation:

5.5 million hectares

Climate:

Tropical savannahs in the south and humid subtropical areas in the north.

Rainfall:

1600–1700 mm

Soil Types:

  • Red and laterite soils
  • Saline coastal soils
  • Poor water quality soils

Agricultural Seasons:

  • Kharif (June–October)
  • Rabi (November–February)
  • Zaid (March–June)

Rice

West Bengal cultivates a wide range of rice varieties, including both traditional (Swarna, Banskathi, Tulsimanjari) and high-yielding (IR36, IR64, Swarna Sub1) types.
Growing Conditions: Rice is grown in both Kharif (monsoon) and Rabi (winter) seasons. Fertile alluvial soil and adequate rainfall support cultivation.
Irrigation: Both irrigated and rainfed rice are prevalent. Traditional flood irrigation and modern drip/sprinkler systems are used.

Jute

West Bengal is a leading producer of jute in India. Both Corchorus capsularis (white jute) and Corchorus olitorius (tossa jute) are grown.
Uses: Jute is used for sacks, ropes, carpets, and handicrafts.
Growing Conditions: Fertile soil and favorable climate support jute cultivation.

Tea

Darjeeling is renowned for its high-quality tea, known for its distinctive flavor and aroma.
Climatic Conditions: Ample rainfall, cool temperatures, and misty conditions in higher elevations create ideal conditions for tea.
Economic Importance: Darjeeling tea is exported worldwide.

Sugarcane

Sugarcane is cultivated in Bardhaman, Murshidabad, Bankura, Nadia, and North 24 Parganas.
Varieties: Both traditional and high-yielding hybrids.
Climate and Soil: Thrives in tropical/subtropical climates with well-distributed rainfall and alluvial soils.
Irrigation: Surface, drip, and sprinkler irrigation are used.
Uses: Sugarcane is processed for sugar, jaggery, and ethanol.
