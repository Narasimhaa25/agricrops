Overview

Manipur, located in northeastern India, has a diverse agro-climatic setting that supports the cultivation of a variety of crops. The state's economy is largely dependent on agriculture, and farmers in Manipur grow both food and cash crops.

Rice

Rice is a staple food and a major crop cultivated in Manipur. The state has a significant area under paddy cultivation, and rice is a key component of the local diet.
Varieties: Manipur cultivates various varieties of rice, both traditional and improved high-yielding varieties.
Climatic Conditions: Diverse agro-climatic conditions due to variations in altitude and topography. Both upland and lowland rice cultivation practices are followed.
Kharif and Rabi Cultivation: Rice is cultivated during both the Kharif (monsoon) and Rabi (winter) seasons.
Traditional Farming Practices: Use of organic manure, manual methods, and traditional rice varieties.
Irrigation: Rainfed rice cultivation is common, but irrigation facilities are expanding.
Landraces and Biodiversity: Local landraces of rice contribute to biodiversity.
Terrace Farming: In hilly areas, terrace farming is employed.
Local Rice Cuisine: Rice is central to Manipuri cuisine, with dishes like Eromba, Chakhao Kheer, and Kangsoi.

Maize

Maize is grown across different regions of Manipur, supporting both rainfed and irrigated cultivation.
Climatic Conditions: Well-suited to the tropical and subtropical climate.
Varieties: Both traditional and improved high-yielding hybrids.
Kharif Cultivation: Often cultivated during the Kharif season (monsoon).
Rainfed and Irrigated Cultivation: Rainfed is common, but irrigation is expanding.
Multiple Uses: Used for food (roti, porridge, snacks) and as fodder.
Crop Rotation: Included in crop rotation to improve soil fertility.
Traditional Practices: Intercropping and mixed cropping are followed in some areas.
Government Support: Support through seeds, financial assistance, and technical guidance.
Food Security: Important for local diet and nutrition.

Potatoes

Potatoes are grown in different regions of Manipur, thriving in cool climates and higher-altitude regions.
Varieties: Various types, chosen based on soil, climate, and use.
Kharif and Rabi Cultivation: Can be cultivated in both seasons, with Rabi common in cooler regions.
Crop Rotation: Included to break pest/disease cycles and improve soil.
Irrigation: Supplemental irrigation is often provided.
Food Security: Important staple, consumed in many forms.
Market Presence: Consumed locally and sold to other states.
Storage: Potatoes can be stored for extended periods.

Soybean

Soybean is an important oilseed crop in Manipur.
Geographical Distribution: Cultivated in different agro-climatic zones.
Climatic Conditions: Well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
Kharif Crop: Primarily a Kharif (monsoon) crop.
Varieties: Both traditional and high-yielding hybrids.
Irrigation: Grown as rainfed, but irrigation is used where available.
Multiple Uses: Seeds for oil extraction and meal for livestock feed.
Crop Rotation: Enhances soil fertility (leguminous crop).
Government Support: Schemes and subsidies for productivity.
Nutrition: Rich source of protein and nutrients.
