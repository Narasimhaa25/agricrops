State Overview

Climate

Variations ranging from tropical to temperate conditions due to altitude differences, supporting diverse crop cultivation.

Soil Types

Red soil (dominant), brown soil in hills, alluvial soil in valleys, laterite soil, and clayey soil in low-lying areas.

Hilly terrain with central valley (Imphal Valley) and surrounding hills, creating diverse micro-climates for agriculture.

Agricultural Seasons

Kharif (July-September) and Rabi (October-March) seasons with traditional farming practices adapted to local conditions.

Agricultural Profile

Manipur's agriculture is characterized by its diverse agro-climatic conditions and traditional farming practices. The state's economy is largely dependent on agriculture, with farmers cultivating both food and cash crops adapted to the unique topographical and climatic conditions of the region.

Terrace Farming

Traditional terrace farming systems in hilly areas maximizing land use and preventing soil erosion.

Organic Practices

Traditional organic farming methods with minimal use of chemical inputs and emphasis on natural fertilizers.

Rice Diversity

Cultivation of diverse rice varieties including traditional landraces contributing to biodiversity conservation.

Integrated Farming

Mixed cropping and integrated farming systems combining crops, livestock, and aquaculture.

Major Crops

Rice

Primary Staple

Rice is the staple food and major crop of Manipur, cultivated extensively in the Imphal Valley and terraced fields in hilly areas. The state grows diverse varieties including traditional landraces and improved varieties.

Seasons: Kharif (June-October), Rabi (November-March)
Varieties: Traditional landraces, improved high-yielding varieties
Cultivation: Upland and lowland rice systems
Traditional Practices: Organic manure, manual methods
Terrace Farming: Employed in hilly areas
Cultural Significance: Central to Manipuri cuisine and festivals

Maize

Food & Fodder

Maize is grown across different regions of Manipur, well-suited to the tropical and subtropical climate. It serves multiple purposes including food, fodder, and is often included in crop rotation systems.

Season: Kharif (June-September)
Varieties: Traditional and improved high-yielding hybrids
Cultivation: Both rainfed and irrigated systems
Uses: Food grain, livestock fodder, traditional snacks
Crop Rotation: Improves soil fertility in rotation systems
Traditional Practices: Intercropping and mixed cropping

Potatoes

Staple Vegetable

Potatoes are grown in different regions of Manipur, particularly thriving in cool climates and higher-altitude areas. They are an important staple vegetable consumed in various forms.

Seasons: Kharif and Rabi (Rabi common in cooler regions)
Varieties: Various types based on soil, climate, and use
Altitude Preference: Higher-altitude regions with cool climate
Irrigation: Supplemental irrigation often provided
Storage: Can be stored for extended periods
Market: Local consumption and supply to other states

Soybean

Oilseed Crop

Soybean is an important oilseed crop in Manipur, cultivated in different agro-climatic zones. It serves multiple purposes including oil extraction and protein-rich meal for livestock feed.

Season: Kharif (June-October)
Climate Suitability: Well-suited to tropical and subtropical conditions
Varieties: Traditional and high-yielding hybrids
Uses: Oil extraction, livestock feed meal
Soil Benefits: Nitrogen fixation as leguminous crop
Nutritional Value: Rich source of protein and nutrients

Vegetables

Nutritional Security

Manipur cultivates a variety of vegetables including both traditional and modern varieties. Vegetable cultivation provides nutritional diversity and income opportunities for farmers.

Major Vegetables: Cabbage, cauliflower, tomato, chili, beans
Traditional Varieties: Local leafy vegetables and indigenous varieties
Seasonal Cultivation: Year-round cultivation in different seasons
Organic Practices: Traditional organic cultivation methods
Market: Local consumption and nearby markets
Nutrition: Important source of vitamins and minerals

Government Initiatives & Support

Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture

Comprehensive support for fruit and vegetable cultivation with focus on post-harvest infrastructure and value addition.

Organic Farming Promotion

Support for organic certification, bio-inputs, and sustainable farming practices leveraging traditional knowledge.

Soil Health Management

Soil testing facilities and customized fertilizer recommendations to maintain soil fertility in diverse terrains.

Water Conservation

Rainwater harvesting, check dams, and micro-irrigation systems for sustainable water management.

Seed Development

Support for quality seed production and distribution of improved varieties adapted to local conditions.

Market Linkages

Development of rural markets and farmer producer organizations for better price realization.

Traditional Agricultural Practices

Jhum Cultivation

Traditional shifting cultivation practiced in hilly areas with crop rotation and forest regeneration cycles.

Terrace Farming

Stepped cultivation on hill slopes preventing soil erosion and maximizing land utilization for rice cultivation.

Mixed Cropping

Traditional practice of growing multiple crops together for risk reduction and optimal resource utilization.

Indigenous Seeds

Conservation and use of traditional seed varieties adapted to local conditions and climate variations.
