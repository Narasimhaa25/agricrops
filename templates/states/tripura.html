Crops in Telangana

Production Trends (2010–Present)

Area under Cultivation:

2.20 crore acres

Climate:

Semi-arid area with predominantly hot and dry climate. The monsoon arrives in June and lasts until September.

Rainfall:

755 mm (29.7 inches) of precipitation

Soil Types:

  • Red Soils (Chelka and Dubba)
  • Black Soil
  • Laterite Soil
  • Alluvial Soil

Agricultural Seasons:

  • Kharif (Vaanakalam, June–November)
  • Rabi (Yasangi, November–May)

Overview

Telangana's agriculture is characterized by a mix of irrigated and rainfed farming, with major crops including rice, maize, cotton, jowar (sorghum), pulses, oilseeds, and a variety of horticultural crops. The state government supports farmers through subsidies, extension services, and market linkages.

Rice

Area under Cultivation: Rice occupies a substantial area, with both irrigated and rainfed fields.
Varieties: Sona Masuri, BPT 5204, MTU 1010, Swarna, and others.
Climatic Conditions: Tropical, with hot summers and monsoon rains.
Irrigation: Canals, tube wells, bore wells, and tanks supplement rainfall.
Cultivation: Both transplanting and direct seeding are practiced.
Government Support: Subsidies on seeds, fertilizers, machinery, and extension services.
Market: Telangana rice is traded domestically and exported.

Maize (Corn)

Area under Cultivation: Widespread in both irrigated and rainfed areas.
Varieties: Hybrids like DKC 9090, DKC 9141, DKC 9191.
Climatic Conditions: Hot summers, moderate to low rainfall.
Irrigation: Rainfed and irrigated; drip and sprinkler systems are used.
Crop Rotation: Often rotated with cotton, pulses, or sorghum.
Utilization: Human consumption, animal feed, and industrial processing.
Government Support: Subsidies and extension services.

Cotton

Area under Cultivation: Telangana is a leading cotton producer, especially in drier regions.
Varieties: DCH 32, Bunny, Mallika, Bt cotton.
Climatic Conditions: Hot, dry, and low humidity.
Irrigation: Mostly rainfed, but drip and sprinkler irrigation are increasing.
Pest Management: IPM, Bt cotton, and biological controls.
Government Support: Subsidies, credit, crop insurance, and extension.
Market: Cotton is traded domestically and exported.

Jowar (Sorghum)

Area under Cultivation: Widespread, especially in semi-arid and arid regions.
Varieties: M 35-1, CSV 20F, SPV 462.
Climatic Conditions: Drought and heat tolerant.
Water Management: Mostly rainfed, some irrigated in Rabi.
Crop Rotation: Rotated with cotton, maize, and pulses.
Utilization: Food, fodder, and industrial uses.
Government Support: Subsidies, extension, and input support.
