Overview

Gujarat has a strong agricultural base, and farmers engage in both food and cash crop cultivation. A little less than half of Gujarat districts are drought prone. Major Kharif crops in the state are cotton, groundnut, sesamum, castor, paddy, bajra, maize, tur, green gram, sugarcane etc. and wheat, rice, maize, mustard, gram, groundnut, bajra etc are major Rabi crops.

Cotton

Cotton cultivation is a major agricultural activity in Gujarat, and the state is one of the leading producers of cotton in India. Gujarat plays a crucial role in India's overall cotton production. The state's favorable agro-climatic conditions make it well-suited for cotton cultivation. Cotton is primarily a Kharif season (monsoon season) crop in Gujarat. It is sown during the monsoon months of June to August and harvested from September to December. Gujarat's cotton production is closely linked to its textile industry. The state has numerous cotton ginning and pressing factories, spinning mills, and textile units that process the raw cotton into various textile products.

Groundnut

Groundnut, also known as peanut, is a significant crop in Gujarat, and the state is one of the leading producers of groundnuts in India. Groundnut is primarily a Kharif season (monsoon season) crop in Gujarat. Sowing typically takes place during the monsoon months of June to August, and harvesting is done from September to November. Groundnut is often grown in rainfed conditions, utilizing the moisture received during the monsoon season. However, in some areas where irrigation is available, farmers may opt for irrigated groundnut cultivation to ensure a consistent water supply. Groundnut seeds are rich in oil, and after harvest, the seeds are processed to extract groundnut oil.

Great Millet (Jowar/Sorghum)

Great Millet, also known as sorghum or jowar, is an important cereal crop cultivated in various regions of Gujarat. Great Millet is primarily a Kharif season (monsoon season) crop in Gujarat. It is sown during the onset of the monsoon, typically from June to August, and harvested from September to November. Great Millet has diverse uses. It is a staple food in many parts of India, used for making rotis (flatbreads) and other food products. Additionally, it is utilized as fodder for livestock. Great Millet is rich in nutrients, including carbohydrates, proteins, fiber, and certain vitamins and minerals. Its cultivation supports food security and nutritional diversity.

Castor

Castor (Ricinus communis) is another significant crop cultivated in Gujarat. Gujarat is one of the leading producers of castor in India. Castor is primarily a Kharif season (monsoon season) crop in Gujarat. Sowing typically occurs during the monsoon months, and the crop is harvested a few months later. Castor is often grown in rainfed conditions, utilizing the moisture received during the monsoon season. Castor oil has diverse industrial applications, including in the production of lubricants, cosmetics, and biodiesel. Castor seeds are crushed to extract oil, and the by-products, such as castor cake, are used as fertilizers.

Wheat

Wheat is primarily a Rabi season (winter season) crop in Gujarat. The sowing of wheat typically takes place from late October to December, and harvesting occurs in the months of March to April. The temperate climate during the winter season in Gujarat is suitable for wheat cultivation. Cool temperatures and relatively low humidity create favorable conditions for the crop. Farmers in Gujarat often adopt high-yielding wheat varieties developed through agricultural research. These varieties are selected for their disease resistance, adaptability, and yield potential. Wheat is a crucial crop for food security in Gujarat, providing a staple food for the population.
