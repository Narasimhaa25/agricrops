Agricultural Profile

Climate

Subtropical climate with hot summers and mild winters. Semi-arid conditions with drought-prone districts requiring efficient water management.

Rainfall Pattern

Annual precipitation of 700-900mm, with nearly half the districts being drought-prone, emphasizing water conservation and irrigation.

Soil Diversity

Six major soil types: Black soil, Mixed red and black soils, Residual sandy soils, Alluvial soils, Saline/alkali soils, and Lateritic soils.

Cropping Seasons

Kharif: June–October
Rabi: November–April
Strong focus on cash crops

Agricultural Overview

Gujarat has a robust agricultural foundation with farmers engaged in both food and cash crop cultivation. The state leads India in cotton production and is a major producer of groundnut, castor, and other commercial crops. Despite nearly half the districts being drought-prone, Gujarat has developed efficient irrigation systems and water management practices that support diverse agricultural activities across Kharif and Rabi seasons.

Cotton Leadership

Leading cotton producer in India with favorable agro-climatic conditions

Water Management

Advanced irrigation systems and water conservation in drought-prone regions

Industrial Integration

Strong linkage between agriculture and textile/processing industries

Major Crops of Gujarat

Cotton

Cash Crop
Production Leadership: Leading cotton producer in India with favorable agro-climatic conditions
Cultivation Season: Primarily Kharif crop sown during monsoon (June-August), harvested September-December
Industrial Integration: Strong linkage with textile industry including ginning, pressing, and spinning mills
Processing Infrastructure: Numerous cotton processing factories and textile units across the state
Economic Importance: Crucial role in India's overall cotton production and textile industry
Value Chain: Complete cotton-to-textile value chain supporting rural and industrial economy

Groundnut

Oilseed Crop
Production Status: Leading groundnut producer in India with significant contribution to national output
Cultivation Season: Primarily Kharif crop sown during monsoon (June-August), harvested September-November
Cultivation Method: Often grown in rainfed conditions utilizing monsoon moisture effectively
Irrigation Support: Some areas use irrigation for consistent water supply and higher yields
Oil Processing: Rich oil content seeds processed for groundnut oil extraction
Economic Value: Significant cash crop contributing to farmer income and state agricultural economy

Great Millet (Jowar/Sorghum)

Drought Resistant
Cultivation Season: Primarily Kharif crop sown during monsoon onset (June-August), harvested September-November
Multiple Applications: Staple food for rotis, livestock fodder, and various food products
Nutritional Value: Rich in carbohydrates, proteins, fiber, vitamins, and minerals
Food Security: Supports food security and nutritional diversity in the region
Drought Tolerance: Important cereal crop suitable for semi-arid conditions
Regional Importance: Significant cultivation across various regions of Gujarat

Castor

Industrial Crop
Production Leadership: Leading castor producer in India with significant national contribution
Cultivation Season: Primarily Kharif crop sown during monsoon months, harvested later
Cultivation Method: Often grown in rainfed conditions utilizing monsoon moisture
Industrial Applications: Castor oil used in lubricants, cosmetics, and biodiesel production
Processing Industry: Seeds crushed for oil extraction with by-products used as fertilizers
Economic Significance: Important cash crop contributing to agricultural and industrial economy

Wheat

Staple Crop
Cultivation Season: Primarily Rabi crop sown late October-December, harvested March-April
Climate Requirements: Cool winter temperatures and low humidity create favorable growing conditions
Variety Adoption: High-yielding varieties with disease resistance and adaptability
Food Security: Crucial crop for food security providing staple food for population
Agricultural Research: Varieties developed through research for yield potential and quality
Winter Cultivation: Temperate winter climate in Gujarat suitable for optimal wheat growth
