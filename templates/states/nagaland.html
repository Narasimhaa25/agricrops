State Overview

Climate

Monsoon climate with high humidity levels. Temperature ranges from 21°C to 40°C with heavy seasonal rainfall.

Soil Types

Alfisols, Entisols, Inceptisols, and Ultisols adapted to hilly terrain and high rainfall conditions.

Farming Systems

Traditional jhum cultivation (shifting agriculture) and modern terraced farming systems coexist.

Agricultural Seasons

Kharif (March-August) and Rabi (September-December) seasons with monsoon-dependent cultivation.

Production Trends (2010–2019)

Agricultural Profile

Nagaland's agriculture is characterized by traditional jhum cultivation and modern sustainable farming practices. The state is transitioning towards organic agriculture while preserving indigenous crop varieties and traditional farming knowledge systems.

Jhum Cultivation

Traditional shifting cultivation practiced by 80% of farmers, being modernized with sustainable techniques.

Organic Mission

State-wide initiative to become fully organic, promoting chemical-free farming practices.

Indigenous Varieties

Conservation of traditional crop varieties like Chakhesang, Longpi, and Tamba rice.

Terraced Systems

Modern terraced farming systems being developed to replace traditional jhum cultivation.

Major Crops

Rice

Primary Staple

Rice is the staple food for the majority of Nagaland's population, cultivated using both traditional jhum methods and modern terraced farming systems. The state preserves numerous indigenous rice varieties.

Indigenous Varieties: Chakhesang, Longpi, Tamba
Modern Varieties: High-yielding varieties for improved productivity
Cultivation Methods: Jhum cultivation and terraced farming
Season: Kharif (March-August)
Water Source: Monsoon-dependent with traditional water management
Cultural Significance: Central to Naga cuisine and traditional festivals

Maize

Food & Fodder

Maize is a significant crop serving as both staple food and livestock fodder. The crop is well-adapted to Nagaland's high humidity and abundant rainfall conditions, contributing to food security and rural livelihoods.

Varieties: Traditional and hybrid varieties
Season: Kharif (monsoon season)
Uses: Human consumption, livestock fodder, processed products
Processing: Maize flour, meal, traditional snacks
Cultivation: Direct sowing in jhum and terraced fields
Economic Role: Food security and livestock sector support

Vegetables

Commercial Crop

Nagaland cultivates a diverse range of vegetables for local consumption and commercial purposes. The state's organic farming initiative has enhanced the quality and market value of vegetable produce.

Major Vegetables: Cabbage, cauliflower, beans, tomatoes, leafy greens
Other Crops: Carrots, radishes, cucumbers, bitter gourd, pumpkin
Organic Focus: Chemical-free cultivation methods
Market Access: Local markets and neighboring states (Assam, Manipur)
Elevation Advantage: Cool temperatures favor temperate vegetables
Support Systems: Farmer cooperatives and market linkages

Traditional & Modern Cultivation Practices

1

Jhum Cultivation

Traditional shifting cultivation where forest patches are cleared, burned, and cultivated for 2-3 years before moving to new areas.

2

Terraced Farming

Modern terraced systems being developed to replace jhum cultivation, providing permanent cultivation areas with soil conservation.

3

Organic Transition

State-wide mission to eliminate chemical fertilizers and pesticides, promoting organic and natural farming methods.

4

Seed Conservation

Preservation of indigenous seed varieties and traditional agricultural knowledge through community seed banks.

Government Initiatives & Support

Nagaland Organic Mission

Comprehensive program to make Nagaland a fully organic state by eliminating chemical inputs and promoting sustainable agriculture.

Jhum Improvement Program

Initiative to modernize traditional jhum cultivation with improved varieties, better practices, and soil conservation measures.

Terrace Development

Financial support for construction of terraced fields to provide permanent cultivation areas and prevent soil erosion.

Seed Distribution

Provision of quality seeds including indigenous varieties and improved cultivars at subsidized rates to farmers.

Market Infrastructure

Development of rural markets, storage facilities, and transportation networks to improve market access for farmers.

Extension Services

Technical support and training programs on modern agricultural practices, organic farming, and post-harvest management.

Organic Agriculture Transition

Chemical-Free Farming

Complete elimination of synthetic fertilizers and pesticides, promoting natural soil fertility and pest management methods.

Certification Process

Systematic certification of organic farms and products to access premium markets and ensure quality standards.

Traditional Knowledge

Integration of traditional farming wisdom with modern organic practices for sustainable and productive agriculture.

Market Premium

Access to premium markets for organic produce, providing better income opportunities for farmers.
