Rice

Growing Regions: Cauvery delta (Thanjavur, Tiruvarur, Nagapattinam), Kaveri and Vaigai river basins, and coastal plains.
Varieties: Samba, Ponni, Kuruvai, Kuruvaikar.
Climatic Conditions: Diverse, from coastal to semi-arid; both irrigated and rainfed; southwest and northeast monsoons are crucial.
Cultivation Practices: Land preparation, seed selection, transplanting/direct seeding, irrigation, fertilization, weed and pest management.
Water Management: Canals, tanks, reservoirs, tube wells, bore wells; SRI promoted.
Harvesting and Processing: Harvested November–February; threshed, cleaned, dried.
Government Support: Subsidies for inputs, MSP procurement, irrigation support.

Millets

Major Types: Ragi (finger millet), bajra (pearl millet), jowar (sorghum), kodo, samai, varagu.
Growing Regions: Semi-arid and rainfed regions across Tamil Nadu.
Climatic Conditions: Drought-tolerant, suited for low rainfall and marginal soils.
Cultivation Practices: Sown during Kharif and Rabi; minimal irrigation; often grown as mixed/intercrops.
Uses: Staple food, porridge, snacks, and health foods.
Government Support: Promotion of millets for nutrition and climate resilience.

Sugarcane

Growing Regions: Coimbatore, Tiruchirappalli, Salem, Thanjavur, Erode.
Varieties: Co 86032, Co 86049, Co 86050, Co 0218, Co 0238.
Cultivation Practices: Land preparation, planting setts, irrigation (surface/drip), fertilization, weed and pest management.
Harvesting and Processing: Harvested after 10–18 months (Oct–Mar); processed in sugar mills for sugar, molasses, ethanol.
Government Support: Subsidies for inputs, procurement at fair prices, support for sugar mills.
Economic Importance: Major source of income and employment.

Coconut

Growing Regions: Coastal districts (Thoothukudi, Tirunelveli, Kanyakumari, Nagapattinam, Ramanathapuram).
Varieties: West Coast Tall, East Coast Tall, Chowghat Dwarf.
Cultivation Practices: Land preparation, planting, irrigation, fertilization, pest management, intercropping (banana, turmeric, pepper, vegetables).
Harvesting and Processing: Palms bear fruit after 4–6 years; harvested year-round; processed for copra, oil, water, milk.
Uses: Food, beverages, oil, cosmetics, medicines.
Export: Copra, oil, desiccated coconut exported; supports food processing and export industries.
