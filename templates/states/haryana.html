State Overview

Climate

Continental climate with hot summers (up to 45°C) and cold winters (down to 0°C). Semi-arid conditions with distinct seasonal variations.

Soil Types

Predominantly alluvial soils in plains, sandy soils in southwestern regions, and clay loam soils in northern districts.

Irrigation

Extensive canal network from Yamuna and Sutlej rivers, supplemented by tube wells and modern irrigation systems.

Agricultural Seasons

Kharif (June-October), Rabi (November-April), and Zaid (April-June) seasons with intensive cropping patterns.

Production Trends (2010–2019)

Agricultural Profile

Haryana stands as a testament to India's Green Revolution success, transforming from a food-deficit state to one of the country's leading agricultural producers. Known as the "Wheat Bowl of India," the state contributes significantly to national food security through intensive and scientific farming practices.

Green Revolution Legacy

Pioneer in adopting high-yielding varieties and modern farming techniques during the 1960s Green Revolution.

Food Security Contribution

Major contributor to India's central food grain reserves, ensuring national food security.

Agricultural Productivity

Highest productivity per hectare in wheat and rice among Indian states.

Modern Infrastructure

Well-developed irrigation network, storage facilities, and agricultural markets.

Major Crops

Wheat

Primary Staple

Haryana is India's second-largest wheat producer, contributing over 12% of national production. The state's fertile alluvial soils and extensive irrigation network create ideal conditions for wheat cultivation.

Season: Rabi (November-April)
Major Districts: Karnal, Kurukshetra, Sirsa, Fatehabad, Hisar
Varieties: HD-2967, PBW-550, DBW-88, WH-1105
Productivity: 4,500-5,000 kg/hectare
Irrigation: Canal irrigation and tube wells
Export Quality: High protein content, suitable for bread making

Rice (Basmati)

Premium Export

Haryana is renowned for producing premium Basmati rice, particularly in the foothills of the Shivalik range. The state's Basmati rice is exported globally and commands premium prices in international markets.

Season: Kharif (June-October)
Major Districts: Karnal, Kurukshetra, Yamunanagar, Ambala
Premium Varieties: Pusa Basmati-1121, Pusa Basmati-1509, CSR-30
Export Markets: Middle East, Europe, USA
Special Features: Long grain, aromatic, excellent cooking quality
Water Requirement: High, supported by canal irrigation

Cotton

Cash Crop

Cotton cultivation in Haryana has expanded significantly with the adoption of Bt cotton varieties. The crop is primarily grown in the southwestern districts with adequate irrigation facilities.

Season: Kharif (June-October)
Major Districts: Sirsa, Fatehabad, Hisar, Bhiwani
Varieties: Bt cotton hybrids, RCH-134, MRC-7017
Irrigation: Drip irrigation and sprinkler systems
Fiber Quality: Medium staple length, good strength
Processing: Ginning and pressing facilities available

Sugarcane

Industrial Crop

Sugarcane cultivation in Haryana is concentrated in districts with assured irrigation. The crop supports the state's sugar industry and provides raw material for ethanol production.

Season: Annual crop (12-18 months)
Major Districts: Yamunanagar, Kurukshetra, Karnal
Varieties: Co-0238, CoJ-64, Co-89003
Water Requirement: Very high, canal and tube well irrigation
Processing: Sugar mills and ethanol plants
By-products: Bagasse for paper and power generation

Modern Cultivation Practices

1

Precision Agriculture

Adoption of GPS-guided tractors, soil testing, and variable rate application of fertilizers for optimized crop production.

2

Water Management

Implementation of micro-irrigation systems, laser land leveling, and efficient water use practices to conserve water resources.

3

Mechanization

High level of farm mechanization including combine harvesters, seed drills, and modern tillage equipment.

4

Crop Diversification

Promotion of alternative crops like vegetables, fruits, and oilseeds to reduce dependence on wheat-rice system.

Government Initiatives & Support

Mera Pani Meri Virasat

Scheme to promote crop diversification and reduce water-intensive crops, providing financial incentives to farmers for shifting from paddy to alternative crops.

Bhavantar Bharpayee Yojana

Price deficiency payment scheme ensuring farmers receive minimum support price for their produce, protecting them from market fluctuations.

Soil Health Management

Free soil testing facilities and customized fertilizer recommendations to maintain soil fertility and reduce input costs.

Agricultural Infrastructure

Development of cold storage facilities, grain markets, and rural roads to improve post-harvest management and market access.

Krishi Vigyan Kendras

Network of agricultural extension centers providing technical support, training, and demonstration of latest farming technologies.

Subsidy Schemes

Subsidies on seeds, fertilizers, agricultural machinery, and irrigation equipment to reduce farming costs and increase profitability.

Crop Rotation Systems

Wheat-Rice System

Traditional rotation system with wheat in Rabi season followed by rice in Kharif season. Provides food security but intensive in water and fertilizer use.

Wheat-Cotton System

Wheat followed by cotton rotation, popular in southwestern districts. Provides both food grain and cash crop benefits.

Diversified Rotation

Wheat-maize-mustard or wheat-sugarcane rotations to break pest cycles and improve soil health while maintaining profitability.

Vegetable Integration

Integration of vegetables like potato, onion, and seasonal vegetables in rotation to increase income per unit area.
