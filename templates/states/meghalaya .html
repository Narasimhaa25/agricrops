Overview

Meghalaya, a state in northeastern India, has diverse agro-climatic conditions, and a variety of crops are cultivated in the region. Agriculture plays a crucial role in the state's economy, providing livelihoods to a significant portion of the population.

Rice

Rice is cultivated across different regions of Meghalaya, with both upland and lowland varieties adapting to the diverse agro-climatic conditions. Meghalaya's climate, characterized by high rainfall and hilly terrain, is suitable for rice cultivation, allowing for both Kharif (monsoon) and Rabi (winter) crops. Upland rice is grown on sloping terrains, while lowland rice is cultivated in areas with better water availability, often using terraced fields. Traditional farming practices, including shifting (jhum) cultivation, are still followed in some areas. Rainfed rice is common, but supplemental irrigation is practiced in lowlands. Indigenous rice varieties contribute to local biodiversity and food security.

Maize

Maize is grown in various regions of Meghalaya, adapting to the state's high rainfall and hilly terrain. Both upland and lowland areas are used for maize cultivation, with both traditional and improved hybrids grown. Maize is often cultivated as a Kharif crop, sown during the monsoon. It serves as a staple food, fodder for livestock, and is included in crop rotation to improve soil fertility. Mixed cropping and intercropping are common traditional practices.

Ginger

Ginger is grown in various regions of Meghalaya, benefiting from the state's high rainfall and moderate temperatures. Both traditional and improved varieties are cultivated, often as a monocrop or integrated into agroforestry systems. Ginger is propagated through rhizomes, planted during the pre-monsoon or early monsoon season. Meghalaya's ginger is well-regarded for its quality and is supplied to regional and national markets, contributing significantly to farmers' income and the state's economy.

Turmeric

Turmeric is cultivated in various regions of Meghalaya, thriving in the state's high rainfall and hilly terrain. Both traditional and improved varieties are grown, often as a monocrop or in intercropping systems. Turmeric is propagated through rhizomes, planted before or during the monsoon. Meghalaya's turmeric is recognized for its quality and is marketed locally and regionally, contributing to farmers' income.

Oranges

Meghalaya is known for its oranges, especially in regions like Sohra (Cherrapunji). The state's high rainfall and hilly terrain provide favorable conditions for citrus cultivation. Oranges are typically harvested during the winter months and are well-regarded for their flavor and aroma. Orange cultivation contributes significantly to the income of farmers and the state's agricultural economy, with produce consumed locally and supplied to regional markets.
